WALLETIFAI SAVING APP

Walletifai intelligently detects bills and subscriptions and helps manage them on a daily basis.

Walletifai website

Role

・Research
・Ideation/wireframes
・User Testing
・Prototyping
・Hi-fidelity design
・Developer handoff

Goal

Develop a money saving app to help people get a better view of their financial life by showing them the most useful insights generated by the AI algorhythm. Helping manage bills and subscriptions with insight into the past and projecting into the future.

Approach

1. Defining MVP features by conducting user research and analysing competitor products.
2. Identifying current user behaviour and pain points, creating user personas. Ideating solutions.
3. Wireframes best ideas, testing and iterating.
4. Creating high fidelity prototypes, testing, iterating and handing design to the developers.

PROCESS

I collaborated with company founders, developers and growth/marketing team to design, test and iterate.

By participating in spint planning and retrospectives I had a chance to have a igh level view of the whole company's interdependencies and priotities, how different teams interact and work together, best ways to prioritize next most important thing and plan according to agile methodology.

RESEARCH

Insights from research were crucial to verify which features to build and when. After gathering as much data as we could while moving quickly, team used affinity mapping technique to sinthesize findings that would be presented to dev and growth teams and stakeholders.

User survey (quantitative)

After brainstorming and reviewing survey questions with the team I created a Google Form that was shared across our channels. Users could share their emails with us to participate in user interviews later.

User interviews (qualitative)

These guidelines helped meß come up with most effective questions:

  • Ask open questions;
  • Understand how user solves the problem, their paint points;
  • Ask about relevant opinions and experiences;

Most of our assumptions were confirmed about pain points that make it difficult for people to save money (paying off debt, large purchases, etc.) and uncovered a few unexpected insights.
